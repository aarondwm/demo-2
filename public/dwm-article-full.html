Saudi Aramco Reaches 70% Local Content Target in Procurement via iktva Program

Aramco's supply chain localization initiative hits milestone, boosting domestic industry and economic diversification efforts.

N
Newsroom
March 26, 2026 4 min read

Saudi Aramco announced on February 11, 2026, that its In-Kingdom Total Value Add (iktva) program achieved a 70% local content target in the company's procurement of goods and services. The program, launched in 2015, focuses on transforming the supply chain by increasing the share of locally produced and sourced items. Aramco stated its intention to raise the local content level to 75% by 2030.

Since its inception, iktva has added more than $280 billion to Saudi Arabia's GDP and attracted $9 billion in inward investment. The program has contributed to the creation of more than 200,000 direct and indirect jobs across the Kingdom. It has identified over 200 localization opportunities across 12 key sectors, representing an annual market size of $28 billion.

The iktva program has played a central role in building a robust local supply chain. By localizing goods and services, it strengthened the resilience and reliability of Aramco's supply chain and reduced vulnerability to global disruptions. The initiative has enabled the local manufacture of 47 strategic products in Saudi Arabia for the first time through investments in new manufacturing facilities.

More than 350 investments from 35 countries have been catalyzed in new manufacturing facilities across the Kingdom, backed by the $9 billion in capital. These developments support broader economic goals by converting project spending into domestic multipliers. The program has stimulated productivity improvements and reinforced supply chain resilience against external pressures such as cost inflation.

"I am immensely proud of the transformational effect iktva has had on Aramco and its positive impact on Saudi Arabia's economy. This announcement marks a major milestone in the program's journey and reflects an important leap in the Kingdom's industrial development."

— Amin H. Nasser, Aramco President & CEO

Nasser added that iktva serves as a core pillar of Aramco's strategy to build a competitive national industrial ecosystem that supports the energy sector, while also enabling broader economic growth and creating thousands of job opportunities for Saudi nationals. He noted that localizing the supply chain ensures operational reliability while mitigating disruption.

In 2025, iktva organized eight regional supplier forums worldwide to connect global investors and manufacturers with localization opportunities in Saudi Arabia. These events complemented the program's flagship biennial forum. The efforts have positioned iktva as one of the leading examples of supply chain-led economic transformation over the past decade.
