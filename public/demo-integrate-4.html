Saudi Aramco Reaches 70% Local Content Target Ahead of 2025 Iktva Deadline

The state oil giant's Iktva programme hits a milestone that reshapes procurement across the Kingdom — and signals what's next for GCC supply chains.

Saudi Aramco has officially crossed the 70% local content threshold under its In-Kingdom Total Value Add programme, a landmark in the company's decades-long effort to localize its supply chain and reduce dependence on foreign contractors.

The milestone, confirmed by CEO Amin H. Nasser at the annual Iktva Forum, comes a full year ahead of the programme's original 2025 target — a timeline many analysts had considered aspirational when it was set in 2015.
